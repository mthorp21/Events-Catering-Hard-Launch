Designed to be easy for the guest and airtight for operations. Every step has a clear owner and a clear action.

The Steps
1
Inquiry
Guest speaks to the GM in the restaurant, or submits an inquiry through the Morning Belle events page. All inquiries route directly to the dedicated Catering Coordinator.
2
Menu Selection
Catering Coordinator works with the guest to build their event from the Morning Belle catering menu. Selections are due 14 days before the event and feed directly into the proposal.
3
Proposal
Catering Coordinator builds the complete proposal — menu, pricing, and agreement terms — and delivers it to both the GM and the guest within 48 hours of inquiry.
4
Deposit
25% of F&B minimum due at signing. No date is held without a signed agreement and deposit. Non-refundable per the cancellation schedule.
5
Event
GM owns the night. We take care of everything. 20% auto gratuity added to the final bill, quoted in advance on every proposal.

Program A
Catering Tasting Event
Invite them in. Let the food close it.
A complimentary hosted tasting inside Morning Belle, available at any of the four locations before 10am or after close. Invitees are the business owners, office managers, and admin assistants from your local drop list — the people who actually place the orders. No sales pitch. Just food, coffee, and a walkthrough of what catering looks like.
The Details
  • •12–20 invitees per event. Intimate enough to be a real conversation.
  • •45-minute format. Arrive, eat, see the menu, ask questions, leave with materials.
  • •GM hosts. Wes or a senior Gardener runs the food. No outside speaker needed.
  • •Serve 4-5 catering menu items. Include the Cinnamon Roll French Toast. Always.
  • •Every attendee leaves with a catering menu, events one-pager, and a 10% off first order card.
  • •Target: one tasting per location within the first 60 days of launch.
Invite Language
"We are hosting a complimentary catering tasting at Morning Belle for a small group of local businesses. Come try the menu, meet the team, and see what it looks like to bring the Belle to your office. No agenda. Just really good food."
Program B
Business Mix and Mingle
After hours. Spouses welcome. No agenda.
An after-hours social hosted inside Morning Belle. Invitees are business owners, managers, and influencers from the local drop list — and they can bring their spouse or partner. This is not a sales event. It is an experience. They see the space, they enjoy the food and drink, they meet other local business owners. Morning Belle becomes the backdrop for community. Every attendee leaves having experienced what a private event actually feels like.
The Details
  • •30–50 attendees. Couples welcome. Casual dress.
  • •6:30–8:30pm, after regular close. Weeknight preferred.
  • •Passed appetizers and a Belle Mimosa bar. Full catering spread on display.
  • •No formal program. Mike or the GM welcomes everyone briefly — 2 minutes max.
  • •Every attendee leaves with an events and catering packet plus a private event inquiry card.
  • •Target: one Mix and Mingle per location within the first 90 days of launch.
  • •Build the invite list from the local drop contacts. Priority: anyone who already received a drop.
Invite Language
"You and your spouse are invited to an after-hours evening at Morning Belle. Local business owners, good food, a mimosa bar, and great company. No agenda. No pitch. Just a chance to connect — inside the restaurant you already love."
Sequencing: Run the Catering Tasting first (weeks 3–4 post-launch). Run the Mix and Mingle second (weeks 6–8). By the time invitees arrive at the Mix and Mingle, they have already received a drop, possibly attended the tasting, and seen the ads. The event converts a warm audience, not a cold one.
